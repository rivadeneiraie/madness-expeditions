Unique biodiversity above 4,000 masl
The area is exceptionally rich in flora, fauna and landscape diversity, not to mention the history of the region's ancient peoples. Endemic birds, lagoons, hot springs and cultural relics coexist at high altitude, offering an experience that goes far beyond the summit.

Volcano · Catamarca

Ojos del Salado

6,893 masl
The world's highest volcano and the second highest summit in the Americas. On the border between Argentina and Chile — accessed from Catamarca by 4×4 truck. A summit that very few people in the world can reach.
7 / 18
Volcano · Catamarca

Pissis

6,795 masl
Second highest volcano in the world and third highest summit in the Americas. It has 8 secondary peaks. Its main attraction: the "Mar del Plata" base camp at 4,700 masl, reached by crossing two large lagoons from which the SixThousanders can be seen in all their splendor.
8 / 18
Volcano · La Rioja

Bonete Chico

6,759 masl
Surrounded by several SixThousanders. Access is via the "Corona del Inca" route, crossing the "Los Aparejos" lagoon at over 4,000 masl. The shelters used are houses built during the time San Martín crossed the Andes.
9 / 18
Volcano · Catamarca

Tres Cruces

6,748 masl
A volcano with three summits — the South Summit is the highest. Accessible from Catamarca or from Chile, where the Tres Cruces National Park is located. An experience that combines two countries in a single expedition.
10 / 18
Volcano · Salta

Llullaillaco

6,740 masl
The volcano farthest from any city. To reach it, two large salt flats must be crossed. Famous for the 3 Inca mummies discovered near the summit — on display at the Museum of High Altitude (MAAM) in Salta.
11 / 18
Volcano · Catamarca

Walter Penck

6,658 masl
A volcano situated in the heart of the Catamarca Puna. A summit of great beauty and challenge, accessed entirely through the province of Catamarca. Part of the most impressive collection of volcanoes in the Americas.
12 / 18
Volcano · Catamarca

Incahuasi

6,640 masl
"House of the Incas" — one of the most visited on the circuit. Located next to Volcán San Francisco, accessed from the province of Catamarca. Ideal for combining in a single expedition with other summits in the area.
13 / 18
Volcano · Chile

Barrancas Blancas

6,260 masl
A volcano rarely visited or summited. Its access logistics are similar to Volcán San Francisco, with entry from the Argentine side. A summit reserved for those who seek the authentic, off the beaten path.
14 / 18
Volcano · Chile

Peñas Blancas

6,100 masl
Located in Chile, also accessible from the province of Catamarca. Rarely visited — a summit for those seeking adventure beyond well-known destinations. An austere and monumental landscape in equal measure.

Volcano · Catamarca

Volcán
San Francisco

6,040 masl
An icon of the region and the first experience at six thousand meters for many mountaineers. Perfect for acclimatizing and preparing the body for more demanding SixThousanders. Accessible by 4×4 truck up to 5,100 masl.
